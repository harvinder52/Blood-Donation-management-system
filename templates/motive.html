WHY BLOOD DONATION MATTERS

The Power of Donation

Every 2 seconds, someone in the world needs blood. Yet only 3% of eligible people donate annually.

Blood donation is a vital community service that saves millions of lives each year. In developed nations, most donors are unpaid volunteers who contribute to maintain community blood supplies. While some countries have established blood banks, others still rely on family donations during emergencies.

Despite various motivations - from altruism to social responsibility - the global donor pool remains insufficient to meet demand. Interestingly, disaster situations often see temporary surges in donations, sometimes creating temporary oversupply.
